OUR VISION

SHARING FOR ALL - INDUSTRIES AND PEOPLE

We believe that the power of sharing can be used to provide better value in all industries, even those that might seem completely obvious. The vision for the Sharetego platform is to become the place for the shared economy in the services sector.

Sharetego will begin with train transport, but our plans are much grander. Hotels, gyms, museums, restaurants, leisure spots and much more are on our radar.

OUR ADVISORS

Lord Andrew Adonis

ADVISORY BOARD

As a former United Kingdom Minister of Transport, Lord Adonis has pioneered large scale transportation projects in the UK such as High Speed 2, and the electrification of some of the UK’s main railway lines. He was elevated to the Peerage and appointed to the House of Lords in 2005. A rail enthusiast, he believes that sustainable transport is the only way forward, and therefore found Sharetego’s focus on sustainability particularly appealing. He has decided to join Sharetego’s advisory board in order to propel the company forward.

Shlomo Ben Ami

ADVISORY BOARD

Professor Shlomo Ben Ami (D. Phil. Oxford) is a former diplomat who also served as Israel’s foreign minister. In the business sector, he served as an advisor in global operations to leading Israeli holdings company Koor. He was also a member of the international advisory board of Gas Natural (now Naturgy). Currently, he is a board member of La Caixa’s Banking Foundation. As a result, Shlomo has an extensive network of connections to top executives and business leaders.

Lord Michael Levy

STRATEGIC ADVISOR

Lord Michael Levy qualified as a Chartered Accountant, developed a successful, professional practice and then went into commerce and built and sold several successful companies.  He was elevated to the Peerage and appointed a member of the House of Lords in 1997.  From 1998-2007 he was the Personal Envoy and Advisor on the Middle East to Prime Minister Tony Blair. He has been involved in the charitable sector most of his life and chaired the Academies Programme in education and he is now President of a number of organisations including Sense, Barnet & Southgate Colleges, Jewish Care, JLGB and a number of others.
